Algorithms & Society
How accurate is software used to predict future criminals?

And is it biased against certain groups?
Risk Assessment Algorithms
In many states, judges, probation and parole officers use algorithms to consider how likely a criminal defendant will recidivate.

Northpointe Inc. created a popular commercial tool called COMPAS.

COMPAS assigns defendants several scores, such as "Risk of Recidivism" and "Risk of Violent Recidivism."
Propublica Investigates
In 2016, journalists from ProPublica (a non-profit investigative journalist organization) gathered data on criminal defendants in Broward County, Florida.

They compared the risk scores predicted by COMPAS to what actually happened 2 years later.
Pretrial Score
Broward County mainly uses the score to determine whether to release or detain a defendant before his or her trial.

ProPublica discarded scores that were assessed at parole, probation or other stages in the criminal justice system.

This leaves 10,192 people who were assessed at the pretrial stage.
Of those defendants, 3,185 or 34% recidivated in 2 years.
